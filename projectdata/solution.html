This is a flowchart of a smart hybrid energy management system using Arduino. The flowchart shows how the system decides which power source to use (solar, grid, or battery) based on the availability of solar power, the grid, and the battery level. The system first initializes and senses the voltage of the solar, grid, and battery. Then, it checks the availability of solar power. If solar power is high, normal, or low, the system will use solar power to supply the load. If solar power is high and the battery level is below 40%, the system will also charge the battery. If solar power is normal or low, and the grid is available during peak hours, the system will use grid power. If solar power is normal or low, and the grid is available during non-peak hours and the battery level is above 40%, the system will use grid power. If solar power is normal or low, and the grid is not available, the system will use battery power if the battery level is high or normal. If none of the above conditions are met, the system will stop.

Solution 2

Sensors can play a crucial role in collecting real-time data to optimize energy usage. For example, occupancy sensors can detect if a room is occupied and adjust lighting and HVAC systems accordingly, saving energy when no one is present. Temperature and humidity sensors can help maintain optimal conditions, preventing unnecessary energy consumption. They can also work in tandem with smart thermostats to adjust temperature settings based on occupancy and weather conditions. Smart plugs with energy monitoring capabilities can be used to track the energy usage of specific devices or appliances. This data can help identify energy-intensive devices and promote energy-efficient behavior. By integrating these sensors with a centralized energy management system, you can gain valuable insights into energy consumption patterns, identify areas for improvement, and make data-driven decisions to optimize energy usage.
